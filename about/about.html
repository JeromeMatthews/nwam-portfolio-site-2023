With all this talk about stories you must be curious, what's nomad's story?

WHY WE DO, WHAT WE DO

We believe that there’s a story to be told in everything done. That in business it’s the story behind the product that drives acceptance and following. Nomad believes in telling those stories, in seeking and developing those narratives with clients. We seek to do this through the use of digital mediums for sharing the story that a client wants to tell, the product they will share with the wider world, or the cause they wish to champion.

Nomad believes in telling those stories. Seeking and developing those narratives. The experience of moving a project from concept from to fruition. A passion for development in all its aspects, this expands beyond the realm of just business. It’s a predominant thought process we adhere too, a way of life that we subscribe too. For us, it’s all about , the in betweenness of getting from A to B and the magic that takes place on the way. We’re all about improvement and growth here, having a kaizen* thought process to our work and lives..


*Kaizen is the practice of continuous improvement.
Kaizen was originally introduced to the West by Masaaki Imai in his book Kaizen: The Key to Japan's Competitive Success in 1986.

Where this came from…

Growing up I had quite a few interests, I was a widely open-minded child who found interests in many topics, I did quite a bit of hobbies that while at the time felt mutually exclusive enough, later started to emerge to me as more and more connected and integrated. My biggest endeavors always involved some discipline, whether purely artistic or physical in nature, there was always a deep interest in the creative and iterative processes that accompanied practiced study of something. I grew to love observing myself rise to creative challenges and developing skill sets in a number of creative practices.

I danced, acted, sang, drew, painted, and practiced creative writing along with quite a bit of other creative indulgencies.
Chief among my interests were technology and media. I sat trying to figure out what was the defining thing that made me enjoy the practice of so many interests, why would it be so intriguing to start anew on yet another project, or script, or rehearsal for a performance, or to aim to hit a new training goal or learn a new acrobatic move or trick and what connected this very visceral feeling I always had in pursuit of them. Something stood out to me. It was how in all instances, regardless of the endeavor, a common theme occurred.

I loved the process of doing these things as much as I loved to do them themselves… The experiences of being able to take an idea about doing something, putting it into practice, and then witnessing how those ideas grew, morph, and mature always fascinated me. That’s when I knew I understood, It’s about the journey , about the in-betweens of experience.

I needed to start with why

I came across a book that also had a major impact on why I chose to use this thought process as the pillar for my company’s statements. “Start with Why” by Simon Sinek.

In it he explains how some of the most successful brands of the world came to be the way they are and why companies such as Apple and Microsoft can dominate cross – industry markets and have such huge impacts on the world. These companies know why it is they do what they do, it’s a belief in what they come together to produce that permeates throughout those organizations, into their products and services and into the physic of their customers. It explains why people trust companies such as those with a level of loyalty that could rival religious fervor in some instances. And for companies that do get their Why right, and have it at the fore-front of their business share similar successes in whichever industry they find themselves in.

When I read this and begin to understand the concepts, I knew that nomad needed to communicate its purpose in the same fashion as well, we seek to speak about what moves us to do the things we do. What matters to us and what we want to contribute to the world while we’re here.

For Apple they want you to “think different”...
For Microsoft it’s all about: “You’re potential. Our passion."
At Nomad I would love to: “Tell your story”...
